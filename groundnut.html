1. Land Selection: Choose well-drained, sandy loam or loam soil with good fertility. Groundnuts prefer slightly acidic to neutral soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0. Ensure the land receives full sunlight.

2. Variety Selection: Select groundnut varieties suited to your region's climate and intended use (oil extraction or nut production). Consult local agricultural experts or your nearest agricultural extension office for recommendations.

3. Soil Preparation: Clear the land of weeds, rocks, and debris. Plow the field to a depth of 15-20 cm (6-8 inches) and then harrow it to create a fine seedbed.

4. Seed Selection and Treatment: Obtain high-quality groundnut seeds from a reputable source. Treat the seeds with appropriate fungicides or pesticides to protect against seed-borne diseases.

5. Planting: Plant groundnut seeds in rows or beds. The recommended spacing varies depending on the variety, but a common spacing is about 20-30 cm (8-12 inches) between plants and 45-60 cm (18-24 inches) between rows. Plant the seeds at a depth of about 2-5 cm (1-2 inches).

6. Fertilization: Conduct a soil test to determine nutrient deficiencies and apply fertilizers accordingly. Groundnuts require moderate amounts of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Apply well-rotted compost or organic matter to improve soil fertility.

7. Irrigation: Provide consistent irrigation, especially during the flowering and pod development stages. Avoid over-watering, as groundnuts are sensitive to waterlogging.

8. Weed Control: Keep the groundnut field free of weeds, which can compete for nutrients, water, and sunlight. Use mulch, manual weeding, or herbicides to control weeds.

9. Disease and Pest Management: Monitor the groundnut crop for signs of diseases such as leaf spot and pests like aphids. Apply appropriate pesticides or integrated pest management strategies as needed.

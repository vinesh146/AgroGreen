1. Land Selection: Choose well-drained, sandy loam or clay loam soil with good water-holding capacity. Ensure the field has good sunlight exposure.

2. Variety Selection: Select cotton varieties suitable for your region's climate and soil conditions. Consult local agricultural experts or your nearest agricultural extension office for recommendations.

3. Soil Preparation: Clear the land of weeds, rocks, and debris. Plow the field to a depth of 20-25 cm (8-10 inches) and then harrow it to create a fine seedbed.

4. Seedbed Preparation: Use a roller or plank the field to create a firm, level seedbed. Remove any remaining weeds, rocks, or clumps from the seedbed.

5. Planting: Cotton is usually sown using a mechanical planter or by hand. The recommended planting density varies by variety and region, but it is typically around 8-12 plants per square meter. Plant cotton seeds at a depth of about 1-2 cm (0.5-1 inch).

6. Fertilization: Conduct a soil test to determine nutrient deficiencies and apply fertilizers accordingly. Cotton requires nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Apply fertilizers in split doses, with the first dose at planting and subsequent doses during the crop's growth stages.

7. Irrigation: Provide adequate irrigation during the cotton's growth stages, especially during flowering and boll development. Monitor soil moisture to avoid over-irrigation, which can lead to disease issues.

8. Weed Control: Keep the field free of weeds, as they can significantly reduce cotton yields. Use pre-emergence and post-emergence herbicides, manual weeding, or mechanical weeders as necessary.

9. Disease and Pest Management: Monitor the cotton crop for signs of diseases such as fungal infections and pests like bollworms, aphids, and whiteflies. Apply appropriate pesticides or integrated pest management strategies as needed.
